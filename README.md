# Ultra-Reliable High-Capacity Wireless Backhaul

**IN PROGRESS - actively being updated.**

Code accompanying our paper on quantifying the reliability–capacity tradeoff of wireless point-to-point backhaul under rain. Using ITU-R rain-attenuation models, it estimates the capacity that links can guarantee at a target reliability level across frequency bands, link distances, and climates, and evaluates multi-band combining and multi-hop relaying as mitigation strategies.

**Status:** Under review at *IEEE Wireless Communications Letters* 

**Authors:** Ufuk Usubütün\* and André Gomes\* (NYU Tandon; Rowan University), Shankaranarayanan Puzhavakath Narayanan and Matti Hiltunen (AT&T Labs Research), and Shivendra S. Panwar (NYU Tandon). \*Equal contribution.



## Code: Estimating wireless backhaul reliability under rain attenuation

**Authors:** André Gomes & Ufuk Usubütün

This repository contains:

- **`wireless_backhaul_publication_result.ipynb`** — the script we used to produce all figures and results in the paper.
- **`experimental_exploration_tool/`** — a separate, AI-generated notebook for interactively testing different parameters. Provided for the convinence of the readers.

All results in the publication were generated by our own script (`wireless_backhaul_publication_result.ipynb`), which we verified for correctness in the cases considered. The notebook in `experimental_exploration_tool/` was **not** used in the paper: it was generated by an AI agent from our script after the work was finalized, its outputs have not been verified, and it is provided only as a convenience for readers who wish to explore. Use it at your own risk.
